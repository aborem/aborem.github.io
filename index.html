Arthur Borém

I'm a first year Computer Science PhD student at the University of Chicago where I work in SUPERGroup and am advised by Blase Ur. I'm interested in usable/mobile/web privacy and security, HCI, and education with a focus on issues of power and harm.

Before that, I worked at Asana as a product engineer for a year. I graduated from Brown University in May 2020, where I studied Computer Science. I worked as a Teaching Assistant for a few courses (CS33, CS112) and was a Head Teaching Assistant for CS18. I also co-created the curriculum for and ran TA Diversity Training for my department.

During my senior year at Brown I worked on a research study with Christina Smith to explore the experience of STEM Teaching Assistants at Brown and craft new institution-wide guidelines for these student workers. I also worked with Jeff Huang, from the Brown HCI group, on developing and studying tools for self-run experiments and with Seny Kamara, from the Encrypted Systems Lab, on ProtestMix, a prototype Android app that uses SMS forwarding and phone swapping to partially anonymize protest attendees in their mobile communication.
